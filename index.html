Bitcoin is the world's first decentralized cryptocurrency that enables secure peer-to-peer digital transactions without the need for a central authority like a bank. Launched in 2009 by an unknown person or group called Satoshi Nakamoto, it operates on a blockchain, which is a public ledger recording every transaction. Bitcoin is scarce, with a limited supply of 21 million coins, and is widely used as both a digital currency and an investment asset.
